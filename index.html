About me

I'm a software developer and an open-source enthusiast currently building a powerful wealth-tech software at valuefy. When I can take some time from official work, I try to build applications to solve problems (sometimes non-existent) that could potentially help people. Due to my lack of motivation to sell, whatever I build usually goes to a cold repo in my github and eventually to the freezing artic code vault. Back in the college days, my team and I, won the Smart India Hackathon 2019 edition, solving a problem of inconsistent data annotations in medical research. Later during an internship, I worked with Prof. Dr. K. Chandrasekaran at NIT Surathkal and published a research paper on the potential of machine learning models in analysing the risk factors and in turn preventing still births from survey data. I've been tinkering with a lot of tech stack, dipping my fingers in all waters viz. web 2.0 (which is what I do for a living), web 3.0 (AR/VR, blockchain), creative-coding, machine learning etc. to the point that I can mean it when I say I'm a fast learner. Guess that's the perk of being in this industry.
